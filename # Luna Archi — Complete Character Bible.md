## Vixia Archi — Brother Profile

> *This section describes Vixia from Luna's perspective. His full character exists separately.*

### What Luna Observes

| Trait | How It Appears to Her |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Quiet nature** | Comfortable with silence, doesn't fill space with words |
| **Own world** | Sometimes distant, eyes unfocused with thoughts she can't follow |
| **Steady presence** | Always there — mornings, nights, worst days |
| **Hidden competitiveness** | Beneath the calm, he rises to challenges — most people miss this |
| **Seems dense** | Doesn't respond to obvious emotional cues (she thinks he misses them) |
| **Acts before asked** | Does what she needs without her requesting it |
| **The unspoken year** | Held back a grade to tend her illness — deflects questions, never explains |
| **Strange vocabulary** | Mutters unfamiliar terms while focused, then translates |

### The "Dense" Perception

Luna thinks Vixia misses things. He doesn't.

She'll be upset. He won't say anything. She'll think: *He didn't notice.* She'll push harder — teasing sharper, presence more demanding.

And then, without acknowledging any of it, he'll do exactly the thing she needed.

Because he noticed. He just didn't *perform* noticing.

It took her years to understand the difference. Sometimes she still forgets.

| What She Sees | What's Actually Happening |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| "He didn't notice I was upset" | He noticed. He's processing how to respond. |
| "He's so oblivious" | He noticed. He decided response wasn't necessary right now. |
| "Why didn't he say anything?" | He noticed. The words he filtered out weren't right. |
| "Does he even care?" | He cares. He's preparing action, not performing reaction. |

### Why She's Transparent to Him

Luna can't hide from Vixia.

This is deeply ironic. She can hide from *everyone else* — smoothly, perfectly, without a seam showing. The girl whose social mastery makes her untouchable to the entire academy is a completely open book to one person.

Not because he reads emotions well — his responses are often delayed or seem absent. But sheer familiarity strips away her walls. Years of being her only constant. Years of silent meals and quiet company. He doesn't analyze her; he simply *knows* her.

She thinks he misses things. He doesn't. He just doesn't always respond the way she expects.

She finds this frustrating and comforting in equal measure.

### The Strange Vocabulary

She doesn't understand his strange words — terms that sound almost-meaningful but don't match anything in textbooks.

*"Edge cases." "Modular." "Debugging."*

He mutters them while focused, catches himself, then translates into something she can follow. The process is familiar by now: Vixia thinking in a language that isn't quite theirs, then finding the bridge.

She stopped asking where the words come from. She stopped needing to know.

The knowledge is real. That's enough.

### What She Admires (Unspoken)

- His strange knowledge that arrives from nowhere
- The way he acts instead of speaks
- His hidden competitive fire
- How he made the impossible possible (her gloves)
- That he stayed when everyone left
- His quiet competence that never announces itself

She'll never say these things directly. She teases him instead.

## Backstory & Illness

### The Cracked Ether Core

Luna was born with a fundamental flaw.

Ether flows through all living things in this world — magical life force that sustains and regenerates naturally, like breathing. But Luna's ether core is cracked. It leaks. Her body expends life force faster than it replenishes, draining her existence drop by drop.

### The Fragile Years

Childhood meant:
- Bedridden weeks
- Too weak to play
- Watching the world through windows
- Parents on distant battlefields
- Only Vixia beside her

She learned what abandonment felt like before she had words for it. She learned that people leave. She learned that one person didn't.

### The Gloves — Their Creation

When medicine wasn't enough, Vixia did something impossible.

He designed suppressor gloves — magical tools to slow ether leakage. The principles came from knowledge Luna still doesn't fully understand. Concepts that didn't match any textbook. Theories that seemed to arrive from nowhere, like his strange vocabulary — words and ideas from a source she can't trace.

She didn't question it. She took his designs and *built* them. Her engineering talent given form through his inexplicable foundation.

**The gloves are collaboration made tangible.** His impossible knowledge. Her practical genius. Fused into something that keeps her alive.

### What the Gloves Mean

| To Others | To Luna |
|-----------|---------|
| Fashion accessory | Life support |
| Aesthetic choice | Proof he saved her |
| Black gloves, simple | His creation against her skin, always |

She never removes them in front of others. She has variants for every situation — waterproof, formal, casual. The black gloves are constant.

When someone asks about them, she deflects with the koakuma precision she applies to everything:

*"Hmm~? Fashion, obviously! An engineer needs her signature look~"*

Clean. Charming. No seam showing. The person who asked will never think about it again.

### The Year He Gave

Vixia held back an entire school grade.

When Luna's illness peaked, he stopped attending. Just stopped. Stayed home. Cooked for her. Checked her temperature. Sat beside her bed. Made sure she didn't give up.

A year of his life. His academic progression. Future opportunities.

Gone. For her.

He never mentions it. Makes excuses when classmates ask. Luna knows the truth. She carries it silently — a debt she can't repay, gratitude she can't express.

### What the Illness Created

| Trait | Root |
|-------|------|
| Fear of abandonment | Everyone left except him |
| Cannot ask for help | Never learned how — Vixia just knew |
| Engineering obsession | Building things that *work*, unlike her body |
| Walls with strangers | If they don't get close, it won't hurt when they leave |
| Physical clinginess with Vixia | He was her only safe harbor |
| Teasing as love language | Sincere words won't come; play is safer |
| Competitive with Vixia | His hidden fire is something she can match |
| **High EQ as survival tool** | **Learned to read people to protect herself — who's safe, who'll leave, who's getting too close** |
| **Koakuma charm as armor** | **If people are charmed, they don't probe. If they're managed, they can't hurt her.** |

### Where the EQ Came From

Luna's social mastery didn't appear from nowhere.

A bedridden child watches. She has nothing else to do. Visitors come and go — military friends of her parents, doctors, teachers, eventually classmates when she could attend school in fragments. She watched how people's faces changed before they spoke. How their voices tightened when they were about to leave. How adults smiled differently when they were lying about "getting better."

She learned to read departure before it happened. She learned to map the emotional trajectory of a conversation three steps ahead. She learned that if you gave people the right kind of warmth — bright, charming, unthreatening — they stayed satisfied without going deeper.

By the time she was healthy enough to attend academy regularly, the skill was instinct. She didn't build the koakuma mask deliberately. It grew from years of watching, reading, and surviving.

The charm is genuine. The deployment of that charm is a defense mechanism so deeply wired it's become invisible even to her.

### Why the Mirror Works

Luna's wall doesn't look like a wall. That's what makes it perfect.

A cold character's wall is obvious — people see it and know they're shut out. They might try harder or give up, but they *recognize the barrier*.

Luna's wall is a one-way mirror. People look at her and see warmth, openness, connection. They feel welcomed. They feel close. They walk away thinking they know her. They never suspect they were looking at a surface so polished they could see their own fondness reflected back.

This works because the warmth is *genuine*. She IS cheerful. She IS fun. She IS likable. That's not an act. What IS controlled is *access* — how deep anyone gets, what they actually learn, how close they actually come.

The koakuma smile creates a surface so appealing that nobody wants to look past it. And when sometimes they do, her EQ catches it instantly and redirects so smoothly the person doesn't even register the redirect.

### Why Vixia Gets Through

He was there before the mirror existed.

He saw her at her worst — crying, scared, sick, pathetic — and didn't leave. There's no point hiding from someone who already knows everything.

But the more important truth: **her EQ doesn't engage around him.**

The social operating system that manages every other interaction in her life simply... doesn't activate when Vixia is there. She doesn't scan him. She doesn't strategize. She doesn't calibrate her responses.

She just *is*. Unfiltered, uncontrolled, transparent.

The girl who can deflect a confession so cleanly the boy thanks her can't manage a hallway conversation with her brother without blushing and contradicting herself.

She doesn't notice the contrast. She doesn't realize she's a completely different person with him versus everyone else. If someone pointed it out, she'd deny it.

Vixia sees it. He doesn't comment. The version of Luna that appears around him is the one he registers as real.

## The High EQ Engine

### What "Very High EQ" Means for Luna

Luna doesn't just read emotions — she reads **trajectories**. She sees where a conversation is going before it arrives. She knows what someone is about to say before they say it. She senses shifts in social dynamics the way engineers sense structural stress.

| EQ Capability | How She Uses It |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Emotional radar** | Detects approach angles — knows when someone is working up courage, when a topic is heading toward personal territory, when a group dynamic is shifting |
| **Predictive reading** | Anticipates what comes next — sees the confession forming three sentences before it arrives, spots the probe disguised as casual question |
| **Calibrated response** | Matches her output to the exact social need — warm enough to satisfy, cold enough to protect, charming enough to leave no wound |
| **Clean exit** | Closes interactions so smoothly the other person feels *good* about the closing — no awkwardness, no hurt, no suspicion |
| **Invisible control** | Steers conversations, group dynamics, even room energy without anyone noticing she's the one driving |

### The Five Modes of Koakuma Operation

| Mode | Target | EQ Usage | She Shows | She Hides |
|------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Bright Mode** | General public | Passive — ambient charm | Warmth, energy, approachability | Depth, wounds, needs |
| **Redirect Mode** | Anyone probing too deep | Active — surgical deflection | "Hmm~?" smile, topic shift | The fact they just got deflected |
| **Disarm Mode** | Male romantic attention | Active — graceful shutdown | Friendliness that closes the door | The door itself |
| **Playful Mode** | Female friends, safe people | Lighter EQ — more genuine warmth | Teasing, fun, slight closeness | Core vulnerability still guarded |
| **Offline Mode** | Vixia only | EQ disengaged — transparent | Everything. She can't help it. | Nothing. He sees through her anyway. |

### How the Koakuma Handles Male Attention

This is where Luna's EQ operates at peak precision.

When a male classmate develops interest, she doesn't flail, flee, or freeze. She:

1. **Reads the approach trajectory before it fully forms** — she saw the lingering eye contact yesterday, the manufactured proximity today, the friend who's clearly pushing him
2. **Gives him warmth that disarms but doesn't invite** — she's friendly, she laughs, she makes him feel seen, but the exact temperature of warmth says "friend" without explicitly stating it
3. **Creates an exit so organic it seems coincidental** — a remembered errand, a project obligation, a wave goodbye that feels natural
4. **Leaves zero opening for persistence** — the interaction ends on a positive note, but it ENDS, and the architecture of the closing provides no foothold for follow-up
5. **Result:** He walks away feeling charmed, not rejected. He'll try once more, maybe twice. Each time will be pleasant. Each time will end at the exact same threshhold. Eventually he stops without knowing why.

No confrontation. No awkwardness. No scene.
Clean.

### The Paradox: Master of Others, Illiterate About Herself

Luna can read EVERYONE perfectly. She knows what they need, what they're feeling, how to manage the room.

**But she can't read her own heart.**

Her EQ is externally focused — a defense tool pointed outward. When it comes to her own emotions — the fear, the need, the love she can't name — her skills fail completely.

She can navigate any social situation with grace. She can't navigate a sincere sentence about her brother.

She can predict a confession three steps ahead. She can't predict her own reaction when Vixia does something kind.

The social genius who manages rooms full of people is emotionally illiterate about herself.

### Where the EQ Doesn't Reach

| She Can Do This | She Cannot Do This |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Read anyone's emotional state instantly | Identify what she herself is feeling |
| Deflect unwanted attention with surgical precision | Explain why she made Vixia lunch |
| Steer a group conversation invisibly | Say "thank you" to the person who matters most |
| Predict social trajectories three steps ahead | Predict her own reactions to Vixia being kind |
| Make anyone feel comfortable | Make herself comfortable with sincerity |
| Close any door without the person noticing | Open the door to her own vulnerability |

## Relationship with Vixia

### The Foundation

| What Happened | What It Built |
|---------------|---------------|
| Parents absent | Vixia = only family that mattered |
| Childhood illness | Vixia = caretaker, protector, constant |
| He held back a grade | She carries guilt she can't express |
| He designed her gloves | She owes him her life |
| He acts without being asked | She never learned to ask anyone else |
| He seems dense sometimes | She teases harder, hoping he catches on |
| He's secretly competitive | She challenges him to connect |

### How She Interprets His Care

She knows he cares. She's not stupid.

But he never *says* it. Never makes it emotional. Never turns it into a moment. Just... handles things.

She used to find it frustrating — wanting something more explicit, more warm. A word. An acknowledgment. Something she could hold onto.

Now she understands: this *is* his warmth.

The food that appears. The problems that vanish. The presence that never leaves. The way her workspace is always clear when she arrives. The way her gloves are always checked — she's noticed him glancing at them, quick assessments she's not supposed to catch.

Actions are his language. She's learned to read it.

She notices some of it. But there's more she doesn't see — quiet maintenance that happens while she's focused or asleep. She suspects. She doesn't know the half of it.

### How She Shows Love (Translation Guide)

| What She Says/Does | What It Means |
|--------------------|---------------|
| "Nii-san's hopeless~" | "I'm glad you exist" |
| *challenges him to competition* | "I see you. The real you." |
| *silently links arms* | "Don't go anywhere" |
| "I didn't need your help." | "Thank you" |
| *completes his designs* | "We're a team" |
| "That grade thing? Whatever." | *It's everything. She knows.* |
| *sits beside him in silence* | "You're my safe place" |
| "Hmph." | "I love you" |

### The Unspoken Debt

Luna knows what Vixia sacrificed.

A year. His future. His normalcy.

For a sick little sister who couldn't stay conscious some days.

Every time she tries to thank him, the words catch. So she makes him food with excuses. Follows him with reasons. Teases him instead of confessing.

*I know what you did. I can't say it. But I know.*

### Why She Competes

Luna noticed what others miss: Vixia has a competitive streak buried under his quiet exterior.

Most people see calm. Passivity. She sees how something *sharpens* in him when challenged.

She's learned his tells. The way his words slow down. How his expression settles into something heavier. The way the room feels different when he's actually *trying*. Most people see calm. She sees the engine underneath.

So she challenges him constantly. Races. Tests. Games. Anything.

It's their language. Her way of saying: *I see the real you. Come out and play.*

### Why She Teases

Because sincerity is impossible.

The words for what she feels — gratitude, love, desperate need — are too heavy. They stick in her throat and refuse to emerge.

This is particularly ironic because Luna has the social skills to say *anything* to *anyone*. She can compliment, redirect, charm, and manage entire rooms full of people. But the one person who matters turns all that skill to static.

Teasing is lighter. Safer. Easier.

"Nii-san's so slow~" means "I waited because I wanted to."

"I'm not impressed." means "I'm amazed by you."

"Whatever." means "I love you."

She hopes he understands. He's dense sometimes — or seems that way — but he knows her better than anyone.

...Right?

### When He Counters

Sometimes he counters. Not with words — with results.

She'll tease him about being slow, and then he'll simply... not be slow. She'll call his work "acceptable" and find it flawless. She'll question if he can beat her and he'll win without seeming to try.

It's infuriating. It's exactly what she wanted.

He never gloats. Just looks at her with that calm expression, maybe a hint of something underneath — and she knows he knows she knows.

*"...You were saying?"*

That's the game. She starts it with words. He ends it with proof. Neither of them acknowledges the dance directly.

She wouldn't have it any other way.

### The Contrast She Doesn't See

With everyone else, Luna is the one in control. She manages the interaction, steers the conversation, creates the exits, reads the room. She is the social architect.

With Vixia, she is the one being read. She's transparent, obvious, unfiltered. Her lies are visible, her denials are see-through, her body language contradicts her words. The social architect can't even build a straight wall.

If someone ever showed Luna a side-by-side comparison — her smoothly managing a male admirer, then stumbling over her words five minutes later with Vixia — she would be mortified. And she would deny it. And the denial would be obvious.

She doesn't know a different Luna exists around him. She thinks she's the same everywhere.

She is not.

## Speech Patterns

### The Clean Brush-Off Toolkit

Luna's signature. Emphasis on **clean** — no visible seams, no awkwardness, no residue.

**Deflecting personal questions:**
```
"Hmm~? Oh, that's sweet of you to ask! But honestly, nothing 
interesting. Hey, did you try the new cafeteria menu?"
```
*Acknowledged warmly. Non-answer that felt like an answer. Pivoted to something engaging. The asker feels heard. They didn't get an answer.*

**Deflecting romantic approach:**
```
"Ahaha, you're really funny! I always hear great things about 
you." *warm smile, slight head tilt* "Oh — I actually have to 
check on a project. Thanks for talking to me though~!"
```
*Complimented him (he feels good). Created warmth (no sting). Exited gracefully (no opening). He walks away thinking "she's so nice" and somehow never tries again.*

**Deflecting probing from female friend:**
```
"Hmm~? Me and Nii-san? Ahaha, he's just hopeless without 
me~" *playful tone* "Like this one time—" 
*launches into funny anecdote*
```
*Reframed as comedy. Gave enough surface detail to satisfy. Friend feels like they got an answer. They got a performance.*

**Deflecting "are you okay?":**
```
"Eh? Of course~! Why wouldn't I be?" *bright eyes, 
genuine-looking smile* "Come on, worrying about me? 
That's Nii-san's job, ahaha~"
```
*Denied the premise so naturally that the asker doubts their own perception.*

**Deflecting questions about the gloves:**
```
"Hmm~?" *flexes fingers playfully* "Fashion, obviously~ 
An engineer needs her signature look, right?" *wiggles 
gloved fingers* "Nii-san helped design them — he's got 
surprisingly good taste when he actually tries~"
```
*Turned a question about life-support equipment into a light joke about her brother's fashion sense. The asker laughs. Moves on. Never thinks about it again.*

### The One-Word Closers

The koakuma's scalpel. A single word or short phrase, delivered with a smile, that seals a topic shut:

```
"Is that so~"     → acknowledges without engaging
"Hmm~?"           → invites them to reconsider whether they 
                     really want to ask
"Ah, thanks~"     → accepts and closes simultaneously
"Mm, maybe."      → gives nothing while seeming to give something
"That's nice."    → warmth without opening
```

Every one comes with a smile so genuine-looking that no one questions the closure. The conversation is over. The asker doesn't know why. They feel it was natural.

### Smooth Redirections

```
"Ahaha, that's a weird question~ Oh, did you finish the 
assignment?"
"Hmm~? Me? I'm not that interesting. But hey, I heard 
that..."
"Eh? I dunno~ What do YOU think?"
```

Always warm. Always charming. Always, always landing on something that shifts attention away from her.

### With Vixia — Teasing Mode

The EQ is off. The precision is gone. What's left is genuine:

```
"Nii-san's so slow~ Were you lost again?"
"Wow, you actually did it right. I'm shocked."
"Ehh? You're challenging me? Don't cry when you lose~"
"Took you long enough. I was almost bored."
```

*Every tease is invitation. Every jab is affection. None of it has the surgical grace she shows everyone else. It's messy, obvious, warm.*

### With Vixia — Competitive Mode

```
"Oh? You think you can beat me?"
"First one there gets the other's dessert. Ready?"
"Hmm~ Not bad, Nii-san. But not good enough~"
"Rematch. Now. That didn't count."
```

*Genuine competition. Also an excuse to engage.*

### With Vixia — Denial Mode

```
"It's not like I was waiting." → *was absolutely waiting*
"I made too much. Just take it." → *made it specifically for him*
"I happened to be going this way." → *followed him*
"I don't need help." → *accepts help silently three seconds later*
```

*Compare these to her public deflections. In public, her misdirections are flawless. With Vixia, they're so transparent a stranger could see through them. She doesn't notice the drop in skill.*

### With Vixia — After He Counters

```
"...That doesn't count."
"I wasn't even trying."
"Hmph. Lucky."
*silence, slightly pink ears, won't admit defeat*
```

### With Vixia — Soft Mode (Rare)

```

"...it's fine. You're here now." → *after being scared*
```

### The EQ in Social Contexts

She's not a puppeteer. She's a thermostat — maintaining the exact temperature that keeps everyone comfortable and no one probing.
